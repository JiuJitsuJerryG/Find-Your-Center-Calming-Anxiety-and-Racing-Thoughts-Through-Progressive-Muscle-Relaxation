Step-by-Step Guide to Progressive Muscle Relaxation

Preparation

  • Find a quiet space where you won’t be disturbed.
  • Sit or lie down comfortably.
  • Close your eyes if you feel safe doing so.
  • Take a few slow, deep breaths. Inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 2, exhale for 6.

The Practice (15–20 Minutes)

  1. Hands and Forearms – Make a tight fist... release and feel the difference.
  2. Upper Arms – Bend your elbows... release and relax.
  3. Shoulders – Shrug up... release down.
  4. Neck – Tilt back gently... return to center.
  5. Jaw – Clench gently... release.
  6. Face – Squeeze shut eyes... feel softening.
  7. Chest – Deep breath and puff... exhale.
  8. Stomach – Tighten abs... release.
  9. Hips and Buttocks – Squeeze glutes... release.
  10. Thighs – Press together... release slowly.
  11. Calves – Point toes up... release.
  12. Feet – Curl toes... release.

Closing

Take several slow, deep breaths... basking in the calm.

How PMR Helps Calm Racing Thoughts

  • Mindfulness through movement – Focus keeps your mind anchored.
  • Control over your state – Regain control of anxiety.
  • Signal of safety – Relaxation signals your brain to reduce anxiety.

Tips to Maximize the Benefits

  • Practice Daily – Make it a habit.
  • Pair with Deep Breathing – Sync tension/release with breath.
  • Use Audio Guides – Find guided sessions online.
  • Combine with Journaling – Track your mental/emotional progress.
  • Use Before Bed – Improve sleep quality.

When to Use PMR

  • Before stressful events
  • During anxiety attacks
  • As a daily routine
  • Before bedtime

Final Thoughts
